Steak Tacos

Description

Steak tacos are a vibrant, flavor-packed dish featuring tender, seared beef—typically flank, skirt, or sirloin—wrapped in warm tortillas and finished with fresh toppings. They combine juicy, savory meat with bright citrus, earthy spices, and crunchy garnishes for the perfect balance of texture and taste.

Ingredients

  • Skirt, flank, or sirloin steak
  • vegetable, canola, or olive oil
  • fresh lime juice
  • fresh orange juice
  • soy sauce or worcestershire sauce
  • fresh garlic cloves
  • brown sugar, honey, or agave syrup/li>
  • fresh cilantro
  • jalapeno pepper
  • chili powder
  • ground cumin
  • smoked paprika
  • dried oregano
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • corn or flour tortillas
  • white or red onion
  • cotija, queso fresco, or monterey jack cheese
  • pico de gallo or salsa
  • avocado or guacamole
  • lime wedges

Steps

  1. Marinate the Steak
    • Whisk lime juice, orange juice, soy sauce, garlic, sweetener, cilantro, jalapeño, and dried spices in a bowl.
    • Place the steak in a large sealable bag or shallow dish.
    • Pour the marinade over the meat, ensuring it is fully coated.
    • Seal and refrigerate for 2 to 4 hours.
  2. Prep the Toppings
    • Dice the onion and chop the cilantro.
    • Toss the onion and cilantro together in a small bowl to create a street taco relish.
    • Slice the avocado and crumble or shred your chosen cheese.
    • Cut additional limes into wedges.
  3. Cook the Steak
    • Remove the steak from the marinade and wipe off excess liquid.
    • Bring the meat to room temperature for 15 minutes before cooking.
    • Heat a grill or heavy cast-iron skillet over high heat.
    • Sear the steak for 3 to 5 minutes per side until it reaches a medium-rare internal temperature of 130°F to 135°F.
    • Transfer the steak to a cutting board and let it rest for 10 minutes.
  4. Assemble the Tacos
    • Slice the rested steak against the grain into thin strips, then chop into bite-sized pieces.
    • Warm the tortillas in a dry skillet over medium-high heat for 30 seconds per side.
    • Stack two warm tortillas per taco.
    • Fill each tortilla stack with a generous spoonful of chopped steak.
    • Top with the onion-cilantro mix, cheese, avocado, and salsa.
    • Serve immediately with lime wedges.
